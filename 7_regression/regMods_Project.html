Executive Summary:

This report was commissioned to examine the relationship between type of transmission and fuel efficiency of a car (miles per gallon). The objective is to analyze predictors of high fuel efficiency and then quantify the relationship between type of transmission and miles per gallon (mpg).
There are two types of analyses performed in this report. The first one is a simple exploratory effort to draw conclusions based on simple vizualizations. The type consists of performing multivariate linear regression in an attempt to quantify the effect on several regressors on mpg.
The following are the results and recommendations based on the analyses:

1. Exploratory Analysis:

The first method used in this exercise was Exploratory Analysis. The purpose of this type of analysis is to find relationships in the data that were not previously known, but it cannot be used alone to predict or generalize (correlation is not causation!).

Observations:

  1. mpg is the outcome, all other variables are possible predictors.
  2. am is the test variable
  3. Cylinders, Displacement, and hp are highly correlated, important parameter in mpg.
  4. Weight is independent, but important parameter in mpg.
